Professional Skills Matrix and Action Plan - Security Risk Management

  • From the learning journey of this module, I have acquired substantial knowledge about cyber safety and risk assessment and their respective management technique. My present and past job duties involve a lot of handling of cyber security matters, with a major part on the dealing with vulnerabilities of various software and hardware. Prior to studying this module, in my previous work I used to simply set a fixed time to complete the remediation according to the severity of the vulnerabilities, without conducting too much assessment on the possible risks. Now I will and have actually applied the knowledge that I learned from this module to facilitate my work. For instance, I have employed the quantitative risk assessment technique to analyze the potential risks so that I got a clearer plan on how to prioritize mitigation activities. This technique has significantly improved my work efficiencies and produced better results. In addition, this module has equipped me with ample knowledge on Business Continuity (BC) and Drill Recovery (DR) site deployment. I now understand the different kinds of deployment criteria, allowing me to choose the most suitable deployment plan for various systems of which their Recovery Point Objective (RPO) and Recovery Time Objective (RTO) requirements might be disparate. Intending to finding out the most suitable DR plan for our case (Pampered Pets), I have further attempted to try out the various cloud platforms and built up application and database (DB) servers on different geographical locations in order to comply with the rules of General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Moreover, I established DB server cluster and tried several synchronization methods and used the application load balance services provided by the cloud platform to test the High Available (HA) architecture auto failover solution. In addition, I simulated different scenarios to figure out different RTO and RPO by manual as well as auto failover so as to meet the requirements.
  • In the team project, I was mainly responsible for identifying and assessing the level of potential risks. This sub-part clearly pointed out the possible cyber threats that might come about, the vulnerabilities and the respective risks. It was a crucial and key part of the project as it set out the foundation and direction on how the issues should be solved.
  • The team had performed excellently in terms of team spirit, efficiency and effectiveness and I have enjoyed working with my team members and felt privileged to have the opportunity to work with professionals like them. In our first team meeting through mobile instant messaging app (Whatsapp), we had been able to quickly put together an outline of the project. We actively exchanged ideas and thoughts and summarized a clear work plan. Each of the members picked and chose the part they felt comfortable with and is best suitable for themselves based on their own ability and experience and worked in accordance to the sequence and timeline of the sub-parts. We continued to discuss and updated each other on the progress of the project. The team demonstrated good discipline as all of us had completed their respective part on time, without the need to chase anybody for submission. In the end the project was consolidated by the team member who is proficient in the organization of report and all members had reviewed and agreed upon on it before submission.
  • In this moment where we are about to finish this module, I deeply recognized the benefit of spending all the hours and effort studying it. All those vague concepts that were used to be in my head have eventually become solid knowledge, providing a sound foundation on practical skills and organizational ability which would greatly assist me with my work at present and in the long run. Although I was the Operational Risk Officer (ORO) of the department, I tended to carry out my duties based on pre-existing organizational guidelines to complete the Risk Assessment (RA) or Business Impact Assessment (BIA) without performing in-depth assessment and analysis. From now on I would adopt the knowledge that I have learned and apply on not just these specific issues but also extend it to the projects that I handle, with an aim to perfect them.
